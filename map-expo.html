Child Care in Cuyahoga County
Alena Sorensen

The Ohio State University

A healthy and right-sized child care delivery system is critical to both child development and a high-functioning workforce. The COVID-19 pandemic and related economic disruptions greatly altered the already fragile and tenuous child care service delivery landscape with unprecedented unemployment, K-5 school closures, an increased demand for essential services, child care workforce illness, and practices and restrictions related to preventing virus transmission. As part of the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation and Urban Institute's "Using Data to Inform Local Decisions on COVID-19 Response and Recovery", this StoryMap, led by Starting Point in collaboration with the Center on Urban Poverty and Community Development at Case Western Reserve University, explores the impact of COVID-19 on the child care delivery system in Cuyahoga County between September 2019 and September 2020 and provides local stakeholders with information intended to help them respond effectively and equitably to the pandemic.

Interactive map
Ohio Syringe Service Program 2008-2020
Pranav Padmanabhan

The Ohio State University

This map shows the locations of all open Syringe Service Programs (SSPs) in Ohio from 2008-2020. SSPs are an important public health intervention that have been shown to reduce rates of HIV, Hepatitis C, and drug overdoses by providing supplies and a safe community to people who use drugs. This map displays the evolution of the accessibility of SSPs in Ohio over time, and can be useful to public health practitioners as well as people who utilize these services.

The Multi-Dimensional Legacy of Sam Cooke
Devin Suttles

The Ohio State University

Throughout the inspirational career of a musician, an activist, and a revolutionary, Sam Cooke's story drifted and faded into the deep vast reality of unknown African American history. Specifically in the wide range of uncovered stories throughout the Civil Rights era. Upon searching for hidden figures throughout history for a project, I came across Sam Cooke and was immediately astonished by how much his soul, music, and spirit is engraved into every step, every cry, and every tear shed by Black people as they continue to fight and overcome the social and racial inequalities of our society. This interactive map captures the artistry of Sam Cooke that is produced by the everlasting Black struggle while preserving the beauty of his legacy as became one of the pioneers of the Civil Rights movement.

The Second Blessing--Columbus Medicine and Health
Courtney Fleming

The Ohio State University

To meet The Ohio State University Health Sciences Library's strategic goals of increasing accessibility to our collections as well as embracing innovation, we actively seek ways to digitize not only our library resources, but also medical history artifacts and archives housed in the library's Medical Heritage Center (MHC). The MHC is central Ohio's only medical history special collections, and includes over 3,000 linear feet of archival materials, over 5,000 artifacts and over 20,000 volumes in the rare book collection. By utilizing the ArcGIS story map platform, we transformed "The Second Blessing: Columbus Medicine and Health--The Early Years," a book written by former MHC scholars-in-residence Charles F. Wooley and Barbara A. Van Brimmer, into an interactive, visual digital experience that is now globally accessible from our website. The digital exhibit was created with support from the Columbus Medical Association Foundation.

Urban Heat Island Stations
Tara Allen

The Ohio State University

My map illustrates the locations of the urban heat island stations that have been set up on and around Ohio State's campus. The purpose of these stations is to monitor the urban heat island effect in the area through the use of sensors that track temperature, relative humidity, vapor pressure, and other factors. I wanted to show the locations of these stations to better inform people in the area about the project.

This map represents a tool I developed to do a word frequency analysis across Ohio municipal codes ( the ones that are available online ), tying the results to the incorporated areas they have jurisdiction in.
