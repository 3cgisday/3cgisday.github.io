3C GIS Day(s) 2021
Cleveland - Columbus - Cincinnati
November 15th - 19th

Save the Date

GIS Day is an annual event for students, staff, faculty, and the broader community to learn more about geographic information systems (GIS) and to celebrate the power of geospatial data, methods, and technologies for answering research questions and solving real-world problems.

Join us during the week of November 15-19 for a virtual program featuring a keynote speaker, lightning talk sessions, and a digital map gallery. A detailed schedule will be available closer to the event date.

3C GIS Day(s) 2021 is a collaboration between Case Western Reserve University, The Ohio State University, and the University of Cincinnati. This virtual event is free and open to the public, and registration will be available soon.

If you have questions about this program, please contact geospatial@osu.edu.

Lightning Talk and Map Gallery Submissions are Open!

Lightning Talks

The 3C GIS Day(s) planning committee invites submissions for lightning talks (~10 minutes) from any faculty, staff, or students affiliated with one of the three organizing institutions. We encourage submissions from any discipline that uses geospatial information in any format, such as agriculture and environmental sciences, geography, engineering, business, health sciences, urban planning, and the humanities and social sciences more broadly. We especially encourage submissions from graduate and undergraduate students who would like to showcase their work through a fun and informal presentation opportunity.

Please submit your lightning talk proposal by Friday, October 15: Lightning Talk Submission Form.

Map Gallery

We also invite any affiliated faculty, staff, or students to showcase their original cartographic work through a submission to our digital map gallery. Submission categories include static maps (e.g., from a conference poster or publication) and interactive maps (e.g., story maps, dashboards, and other web mapping applications). Help us highlight the great geospatial projects happening across our institutions!

Please submit your map gallery entry by Friday, November 5: Map Gallery Submission Form.
